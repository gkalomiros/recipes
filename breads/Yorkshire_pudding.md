# Yorkshire Pudding

## Ingredients

* 4 large eggs
* 1 cup AP flour
* 3/4 cup whole milk
* 1/2 tsp kosher salt
* 1 1/2 tsp water
* 1/2 cup beef fat, lard, shortening, or vegetable oil

## Directions

1. Whisk together eggs, flour, milk, water, and salt in a small mixing bowl until smooth
2. Allow batter to rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. Ideally, rest in an airtight container from overnight up to three days in the refrigerator. Remove from refrigerator whole heating oven to allow to return to room temperature.
3. Preheat oven to 450&deg; with rack in the center of the oven.
4. Divide fat evenly among two 6-well popover tins or one standard 12-well muffin tin.
5. Preheat the tin in the oven until smoking hot, about 10 minutes
6. Transfer tin(s) to heat-proof surface and divide batter evenly, filling each between 1/2 and 3/4 full
7. Return tin(s) to oven
8. Bake about 25 minutes, until quadrupled in size and deep brown all over. They should be crispy and hollow sounding.
9. Serve immediately or allow to cool thoroughly before storing airtight in the freezer for up to 3 months.

## Notes

* Best results happen when the batter is allowed to rest overnight in the refrigerator
* If using skim or low-fat milk, replace the water with more milk

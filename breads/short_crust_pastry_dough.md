# Short-crust Pastry Dough

Makes enough for about 8 hand pies.

## Ingredients

* 2 1/2 cups AP flour
* 1 tsp salt
* 2 tbs sugar (optional)
* 1/2 to 1 cup baking fat
* 1/3 cup water

## Directions

1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, and salt
2. Pinch the butter into the flour until it forms a mealy texture
3. Slowly and gently incorporate the cold water just until a dough forms
4. Kneed gently once or twice to fully incorporate the dough
5. Flatten to a disc, wrap, and refrigerate to rest at least an hour

## Reference

*An Unexpected Cookbook* page 29

## Notes

* The colder the butter and water are, the shorter the dough will be, which is good for pies, tarts, and small pies.
  Room temperature butter (and water) will result in a tougher dough, which may be more suitable for standing or
  travel pies. If using cold butter, cut into 1/3-1/2" cubes.
* The fat used can make a lot of difference. Today, butter is the default, but lard can be added or used entirely
  to make a crisper texture. Vegetable shortening can be substituted to make the dough vegan.
* The ratio of flour to fat can be tinkered with. A ratio of about 2:1 will result in a very short dough, adding
  less fat, down to 4:1, will result in a tougher dough.
* How much the dough is worked will also alter how short the dough is since the more the dough is worked, the more
  gluten is developed, resulting in more chew than crumble.
* Pastry dough can be kept up to 2 days in the refrigerator, but can also be frozen for longer periods.
* Traditionally, sugar is not added for savory dishes.
